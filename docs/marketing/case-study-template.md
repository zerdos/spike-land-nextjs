### The Challenge

```markdown
## The Challenge

[3-4 paragraphs describing the problem they faced before spike.land]

**Structure:**

1. Primary pain point (the biggest problem)
2. Secondary pain points (supporting issues)
3. What they tried before (failed solutions)
4. Why those solutions didn't work
5. The "breaking point" moment (what made them look for spike.land)

**Questions to answer:**

- What was broken in their current process?
- What tools were they using? Why weren't those working?
- What was the cost of NOT solving this problem? (time, money, stress, missed
  opportunities)
- What specific trigger made them decide to find a solution?

**Example:**

Sarah's biggest challenge was time. Each client required content across 3-4
platforms. She spent 3-4 hours per day just drafting posts, checking them
against brand guidelines, and scheduling them across different tools.

She tried using Buffer and Hootsuite, but they didn't help with content
creation - just scheduling. She was still staring at blank text boxes, trying
to write on-brand content for 4 different companies with 4 different voices.

By the time she finished her daily content creation, she had no time left for
strategy, client calls, or taking on new business. She was stuck at 4 clients
because she literally couldn't manage more.

The breaking point came when she had to turn down two high-paying clients in
the same week because she didn't have capacity.
```

### The Solution

```markdown
## How spike.land Helped

[3-4 paragraphs describing how they use spike.land to solve their problem]

**Structure:**

1. Initial setup (what they did first)
2. Key features they use (be specific)
3. Workflow changes (how their day-to-day changed)
4. Learning curve (was it easy to adopt?)

**Questions to answer:**

- What features do they use most?
- How did they configure spike.land for their needs?
- What was their onboarding experience like?
- How long before they saw results?
- What surprised them about the solution?

**Include:**

- Specific feature names (AI brand voice, multi-platform scheduling, A/B
  testing, etc.)
- Workflow descriptions (step-by-step of how they use it)
- Screenshots if available (dashboard, content generation, analytics)
- Direct quotes from customer about the experience

**Example:**

Sarah started by connecting all her clients' social accounts to spike.land. She
configured a separate brand voice profile for each client, feeding the AI
examples of past posts that worked well.

The first week, she spent time training the AI. She'd generate drafts, edit
them to match the brand perfectly, and the AI learned from her edits.

By week two, the AI was producing content that needed minimal editing. Sarah's
workflow changed dramatically:

1. Morning: Review AI-generated content for all clients (30 minutes)
2. Edit as needed (15 minutes per client)
3. Schedule with optimal timing suggestions (10 minutes)
4. Done by 10 AM instead of 2 PM

The A/B testing feature was a game-changer. Instead of guessing what content
would perform, spike.land would test variations and learn what resonated with
each audience.

"I went from spending 4 hours a day on content to spending 1 hour. And the
content is BETTER because the AI learned what actually works, not what I think
will work." - Sarah
```

## Case Study Collection Process

### How to Gather Case Study Information

**Step 1: Identify Candidate**

Look for users who:

- Have been using spike.land for at least 30 days
- Show measurable results (time saved, growth metrics, engagement increase)
- Are willing to share their story publicly
- Represent one of our target personas

**Step 2: Reach Out**

Email template:

```
Subject: Would you be interested in a case study feature?

Hi [Name],

I've been following your progress with spike.land and I'm impressed! You've [specific achievement].

We're creating case studies to show how creators/businesses are using spike.land to grow. Would you be interested in being featured?

It would involve:
- 30-minute interview (I'll ask you questions, you share your story)
- A few screenshots/metrics (optional, can be anonymized)
- Review of the final draft before publishing

In return, we'll:
- Feature you on our website and blog
- Share your story across our social channels
- Send traffic/exposure to your business
- Give you 10,000 bonus tokens as a thank you

Interested? Just reply and I'll send over some times for a quick chat.

Thanks!
Zoltan
```

**Step 3: Interview**

Interview questions:

1. What were you doing before spike.land?
2. What was the biggest challenge you faced?
3. What made you try spike.land?
4. What features do you use most?
5. How has your workflow changed?
6. What results have you seen? (specific metrics)
7. What surprised you about spike.land?
8. What advice would you give someone starting out?
9. Can we share your name/company? Or would you prefer anonymity?
10. Do you have any metrics/screenshots you can share?

**Step 4: Draft Case Study**

Use the template above to write the case study. Focus on:

- Specific, measurable results
- Real workflow examples
- Honest challenges (including learning curve)
- Actionable lessons for readers

**Step 5: Review & Approve**

Send draft to customer for review. Make edits based on their feedback. Get
written approval before publishing.

**Step 6: Publish & Promote**

- Publish to spike.land blog
- Share on social media
- Email to relevant customer segments
- Add to marketing materials (Product Hunt, sales pages, etc.)
- Thank the customer publicly

## Metrics to Collect

For every case study, try to gather:

### Time Metrics

- Hours per week spent on social media (before/after)
- Time saved per post
- Time to onboard new client (for agencies/freelancers)

### Growth Metrics

- Follower/subscriber growth
- Website traffic from social
- Email list signups attributed to social
- Number of clients (for freelancers)

### Engagement Metrics

- Average engagement rate (before/after)
- Best-performing post comparison
- Comments/replies per post
- Shares/saves per post

### Business Metrics

- Revenue increase
- Client retention rate
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) from social
- Return on investment (ROI)

### Qualitative Metrics

- Work-life balance improvement
- Stress level
- Confidence in marketing
- Ability to scale

## Legal & Compliance

### Testimonial Requirements

- Get written permission to use customer's name, company, and testimonial
- Allow customer to review and approve final case study before publishing
- Offer anonymization option (Customer A, "A freelance marketer", etc.)
- Don't make false claims or exaggerate results

### Compensation Disclosure

If offering bonus tokens or compensation for case study participation, disclose
this in the case study.

Example footnote:

```
*Customer received bonus tokens for participating in this case study. Results shown are accurate and verified.
```
